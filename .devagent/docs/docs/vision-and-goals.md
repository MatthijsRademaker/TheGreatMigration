# Vision & Goals

## Product Vision

**The Great Migration** is a lightweight personal planning app for coordinating the practical work involved in moving between houses.

The app helps a household organizer turn messy handwritten planning into a clear shared dashboard. It focuses on answering three practical questions:

- What needs to be done?
- Who is available?
- How many people are needed for each job?

The product is intentionally simple and practical. It is not a complex project management platform, and it does not include authentication, email invites, roles, permissions, or production-scale collaboration features.

## Primary User

The primary user is a single household organizer who can freely manage the plan.

They can:

- Create and edit tasks
- Add people as free-form names
- Set priorities and statuses
- Track daily availability
- Assign people to jobs
- Schedule work across multiple days
- Maintain practical move notes

## Dashboard Focus

The app is built around a dashboard that shows the full moving plan at a glance.

Core dashboard areas:

- **Task backlog** for unscheduled or not-yet-planned jobs
- **People availability overview** showing who is available on each day
- **Daily schedule board** with day-by-day task columns
- **Priority and status labels** for quick scanning
- **Summary cards** for high-priority tasks, available people, unassigned jobs, and room progress
- **Move notes** for reminders, deadlines, supplies, and move-day checklist items

## Core Capabilities

The app should support:

- Creating and managing tasks
- Setting task priority
- Defining how many people a task needs
- Assigning free-form people to jobs
- Tracking people availability by day
- Planning work across the full move timeline
- Keeping move-related notes in one place

## Success Criteria

Success means everyone involved can easily understand:

- What they are supposed to do
- When they are needed
- Which tasks are most important
- Which jobs are unassigned or under-staffed
- How the overall move plan is progressing

The experience should feel calm, organized, approachable, and easy to adjust during a stressful move.

## Design Direction

The visual direction should be simple, practical, and green/nature-inspired, reflecting the idea of migration toward a new home in a greener area.
